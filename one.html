Turtles

Like a lot of 80s kids, my first introduction to programming was a toy called Big Trak. It was a truck with a keypad on it's back, and it could be programmed to follow a route...3 forward, turn right, reverse 5 and so on. I didn't know at the time, but this was similar to a Turtle, a programming method whereby a 'pen' is programmed to draw lines on a cartesian plane. Programming a truck is cool an' all, but turtles can draw pictures, which is better.

To work, a turtle needs three attributes: a location (generally x,y co-ordinates on a plane), an orientation (angle) and a pen.

The pen also has a number of attributes such as colour, width, pen up or down (i.e. draw or don't draw).

Turtle also needs a couple of actions: to move the turtle and rotate the orientation. Two actions shoudl be enough to do just about anything:

  • forward(distance) - moves turtle forward by a distance
  • rotate(angle) - rotates orientation by set angle.

But other actions can be built on top of those to simplify things when playing with the turtle.

And that's basically it. Though to implement this in javascript and actually draw shit I'm going to need to set up a canvas and add some line drawing type functionality.
